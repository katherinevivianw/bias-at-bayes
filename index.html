Keeping Bias at Bay(es):

A Naive Bayes Exploration on Retaining
Diverse Talent in the Tech Industry

Katherine Wong, '25
CS109 Probability Challenge Project

Motivators & Background Info

The technology industry's workforce diversity gap is a long-time problem that many have attempted to combat. Less than a quarter of employees in computing and engineering are women; furthermore, only 5% of engineering jobs are comprised of Black workers, while 9% of engineering are Hispanic (Fry 2021).

This project is inspired by a discussion that I had in my seminar course FEMGEN 147: Feminism and Technology, which addressed the following issue. Current solutions for increasing diversity in the tech industry workforce — such as the annual Grace Hopper Conference and diversity programs (e.g. Microsoft Explore and Meta University) — are often hyper-fixated on increasing the pipeline of initially helping marginalized individuals land roles.

However, hiring is just one part of the equation. Largely neglected when evaluating the diversity gap problem is the retention of diverse talent — and what factors might influence an underrepresented employee's decision to leave a company.

What does this tool do?

The goal of Keeping Bias at Bay(es) is to make a user-friendly, accessible tool that utilizes machine learning to demonstrate what factors influence an employee's attrition status, and how attrition status can impact other factors.

The way it works is simple. Behind the scenes, a Naive Bayes machine learning algorithm is trained on a company's Human Resources dataset. The user can input what they want to get the probability of, along with what factors they want to condition on in the UI. Finally, the Naive Bayes algorithm runs with these inputs and outputs the final probability for the user to view.

Since company HR datasets are often not published to the general public, we see this tool being internally used by tech companies to evaluate whether the problem of not retaining diverse talent is evident, and what factors they can consider in order to address the issue.
